Journaling

Journaling has a plethora of health benefits. And it's not just for health. It (anecdotally) is a huge booster to productivity too. It's simple, it's cheap, it's immediate. Let's try it out, and see if it treats us better than Facebook.

Typically, writing it by hand is better than typing, but we're not here to be perfect. We're here to be just a little bit better than before. So typing will do.

It's going to be weird to go to a site to consume some content and then end up writing, but this is healthy and helpful and puts you first.

A note on privacy

This will get easier over time. If you like it, I encourage you to make it a more permanent part of your life.

Privacy

One of the main strengths of journaling is that it's private. Nobody else will read what you write, so you can write whatever you want. Here, I'm asking you to write it on the internet, a place so notoriosly un-private that [add a metaphor here].

I understand your concern. I really do. I don't record what's written here. There's not even a button to submit the journal. I do not want to invade your privacy. I also know it's not that easy to believe me.

But journaling is really good for you, and I would hate for you to miss out on this activity because of privacy. So open up your favorite notes app. Open up a physical journal, if you have one. Write it somewhere, just write.
